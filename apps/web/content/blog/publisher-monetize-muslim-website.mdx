If you run a Muslim website — whether it's an Islamic blog, Quran learning platform, halal recipe site, or Muslim news outlet — you've faced a familiar dilemma: how do you monetise your audience without serving them ads for alcohol, gambling, or worse?

Google AdSense is the default choice for most publishers, but it's notorious for serving inappropriate ads on Islamic content sites. This guide explores your options.

## The Problem With Generic Ad Networks

Google AdSense uses contextual and behavioural targeting — meaning it shows ads based on what it thinks will get clicks, not what's appropriate for your audience. The result?

- Islamic blogs served ads for betting sites
- Quran learning apps showing alcohol brand ads
- Muslim recipe websites displaying ads for haram food products

Many Muslim publishers have reported these exact issues. The standard fix — manually blocklisting advertisers — is a constant whack-a-mole game that never fully works.

## Option 1: Direct Advertising Deals

Reaching out to halal brands directly is the most lucrative option if you have significant traffic. A website with 100,000+ monthly visitors can command meaningful sponsorship fees from halal food brands, Islamic finance companies, and modest fashion retailers.

**Pros:** Highest revenue, complete control over what's advertised  
**Cons:** Time-consuming, requires a sales process, hard to scale

## Option 2: Affiliate Marketing

Halal affiliate programmes exist for Islamic finance products, halal travel agencies, Islamic bookstores, and Muslim fashion brands.

**Pros:** Passive income once set up, fully halal  
**Cons:** Lower RPM than display advertising, limited to specific niches

## Maximising Your Earnings

Regardless of which monetisation method you choose, these tactics will increase your revenue:

### Ad placement matters
Above-the-fold placements consistently outperform. A 300×250 rectangle in the first screenful of content can deliver 3-5× the RPM of a footer placement.

### Mobile optimisation is critical
Over 70% of Muslim web traffic in Southeast Asia and the Middle East comes from mobile devices. Ensure your ad units are responsive and your site loads quickly on 4G connections.

### Content quality drives RPM
Advertisers pay more for placements on authoritative, high-quality content. Detailed Islamic guides, comprehensive halal product reviews, and well-researched educational articles attract premium CPM rates.

### Build an email list
Direct newsletter audiences are valuable for direct sponsorship deals. A list of 10,000 engaged Muslim readers is worth significantly more than 100,000 anonymous pageviews.
